Situated at the southern end of Chicago’s Bungalow Belt, Park Manor's streets house a history of social transformation and architecture emblematic of the hopes associated with home ownership.

The proposed residential program seeks to maintain the ideals fundamental to the success of the Chicago bungalow while reconfiguring the spatial translation of these values to suit a contemporary society.

Specifically, the Chicago bungalow is a successful mediator between two thresholds: a) between private and public on both a micro, interior level and as part of the urban fabric on a macro level and b) between natural and built environment. The proposed design retains these objectives, but modifies the design to encourage both the house and the neighborhood to become more welcoming towards all home-seekers, healing in the process the issue of neighborhood exclusivity that has marked too much of Chicago’s history.
