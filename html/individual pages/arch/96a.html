PUNCTUATED EQUILIBRIUM

CLOSE

A raised pavilion that integrates vertically into its natural environment as a complementary but distinctive structure. As a form, the pavilion follows a trefoil arrangement of similar lobes, each one composed of a vertical set of tweened curves that waffles with horizontal components before concentrating into a dense, central node. Each lobe is locationally situated according to both the height of the lobe and of the topography of the site, with lobe height inversely related to ground elevation.

As a space, the pavilion becomes both a canopy for ground-level engagement as well as a raised tier with access to a previously unreachable elevation. The bottom level is a cohesive space that promotes interactions, while the second level is a series of private units that allow for personal contemplation and immersion with the surrounding foliage. Circulation occurs following the spiral nature of the trefoil and facilitates both horizontal movement on the levels and vertical movement through the stairs.
