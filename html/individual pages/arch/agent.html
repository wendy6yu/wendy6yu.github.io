HARVARD SQUARE INTERVENTION

CLOSE

Perceptually extending space past its innate boundaries through a mixed methodology of a) diagonal scaling and translations and b) forced perspective.

Specifically, stairs are paired with scaled, "shadow" stairs to produce diagonals which extend the space. All stairs are similar, however, the scaling informs users of how each might be occupied. Within every cubic space, the paired stairs are organized such that if the cube is cut from the front, the halves look to be translations of one another; cut from the side, the halves are mirrors of each other.

Stepwells cut into the ground beneath the primary building to form an interactive public program. By hovering the building slightly above street level, viewers are decieved of the depth of space that exists beneath the building.

Tools - Rhino, Illustrator, Photoshop
