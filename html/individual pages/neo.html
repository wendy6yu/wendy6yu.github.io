neo

Neo, as in classical, and less commonly as in Renaissance and Baroque.

Chiaroscuro, as in Caravaggio, and as defined by the exaggerated balance of light and dark for dimension, focus, and its evocative abilities.

The Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum was transportive that day. The architecture of the inside out Venetian Palazzo is always wonderful, but something about the 2pm light was so divine right then.

I watched the beams wash through the arches, painting through the lens an instant Caravaggio.
