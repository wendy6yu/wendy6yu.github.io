60%

1 / 2020

This collection consists of images from Bogotà, Cartagena, and Medellin - the old, the timeless, and the new.

60% of the Colombian labor force consists of informal workers. Unregulated, unprotected, and with no benefits or security.

These workers are more importantly, however, kind, welcoming, and giving. To us, the givers of directions, of smiles, of free souvenirs and blessings, and of a memorable invitation into a home for fresh guava juice.

This is a generous, optimistic country.

On New Year's Day, we saw those clouds (THE clouds), and there I knew in all my athiesm that something, indeed something, was looking over this country.

ABC

11 / 2019

American-born Chinese. Chinese American?

Regardless, there's always a gap between the two words - a space, yes, or maybe a boundary or border. It's quite impressive, actually, how we go from one to the next, and back and forth, and mix them together. Assimilation is, after all, a continuous spectrum.

And consumption of culture? Oh, America loves to consume, and it's got a big mouth too. I eat Chinese food - we all do. We just ate so much takeout, but all I learned was 苹果 from my fortune cookie.

NEO

9 / 2020

Neo, as in classical, and less commonly as in Renaissance and Baroque.

Chiaroscuro, as in Caravaggio, and as defined by the exaggerated balance of light and dark for dimension, focus, and its evocative abilities.

The Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum was transportive that day. The architecture of the inside out Venetian Palazzo is always wonderful, but something about the 2pm light was so divine right then.

I watched the beams wash through the arches, painting through the lens an instant Caravaggio.
